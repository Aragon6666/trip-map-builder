# 2606 Changsha Research

## Planning Conclusion

Stay near Wuyi Square / Huangxing Square / Xiangjiang Middle Road. This keeps the first evening, snacks, Chayan Yuese, Taiping Old Street, Pozi Street, IFS and Du Fu Pavilion walkable, while D2 and D3 can be handled by metro or short taxi rides.

The route is intentionally loose:

- D1: Arrive, check in, light walk around Wuyi / Huangxing / Taiping, drink Chayan Yuese.
- D2: Hunan Museum as the main indoor anchor, then Kaifu Temple or Wuyi area rest, evening Du Fu Pavilion / Xiangjiang night view.
- D3: Yuelu Mountain / Yuelu Academy in the morning, Orange Isle in late afternoon or evening.
- D4: No far points. Breakfast, one nearby city walk, airport.

## Hotel Area

Recommended search areas:

1. Wuyi Square / Huangxing Square / IFS
   - Best for first-time Changsha, Chayan Yuese density, food, night walks and metro access.
   - Risk: weekend noise and higher prices. Read recent reviews for sound insulation and elevator wait.
2. Xiangjiang Middle Road
   - Slightly calmer, close to river, Du Fu Pavilion and Orange Isle views.
   - Risk: fewer cheap options right next to the busiest food streets.
3. Furong Square / Yingbin Road
   - More practical and often better value, still close by metro/taxi.
   - Risk: slightly less "step outside and play" feeling.

Booking checklist:

- Filter recent reviews by "clean", "smell", "soundproof", "elevator", "air conditioner", "humid".
- Prefer cancellable chain hotels or stable local hotels with many recent reviews.
- Avoid basement-style rooms, windowless rooms, and very cheap hotels inside old commercial buildings.

Suggested hotel brands/levels to compare rather than blindly book:

- All Seasons, Atour Light / Atour, Orange, Hanting Youjia, CitiGO, Crystal Orange.
- Choose the specific branch only after checking recent reviews and walking distance to metro.

## Food Strategy

Non-spicy first:

- Breakfast/rice noodles: order yuan tang fen, rou si fen, niu rou fen with "bu la / la jiao ling fang".
- Xiang cuisine: ask for "wei la or bu la", order steamed egg, stir-fried greens, soup, braised dishes, sugar-oil snacks, rice noodles.
- Snacks: sugar-oil baba, tangyou tuotu, stinky tofu only if no long queue and spice can be controlled.

Avoid as anchors:

- Long-queue internet-famous Xiang cuisine restaurants.
- Restaurants where the core dish is heavy spicy crawfish, spicy stir-fry, or late-night bar food.
- Super Wenheyou as a meal anchor. It can be a photo/walk-by point if nearby, but not worth a long wait.

Chayan Yuese strategy:

- Do not queue at one famous branch. Wuyi Square, Huangxing Road, IFS, Taiping Old Street and mall areas have dense branches.
- Use it as a rest stop: buy when passing a short-queue branch.
- Good map search terms: "茶颜悦色 五一广场", "茶颜悦色 黄兴广场", "茶颜悦色 IFS", "茶颜悦色 橘子洲".
